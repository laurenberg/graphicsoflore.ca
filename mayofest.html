Mayofest Branding

Mayofest is the not-for-profit go-to place for camping, music, and general good times. When they announced they were starting a contest to find a new logo I couldn't wait to participate, and show them what a brand can do for their vision.

When thinking of a way to visualize Mayofest, I look back on my time there this summer.

The once cottage party has truly taken on an identity of its own. It’s the most spirited, wild, and welcoming festival myself—and many others—have ever been a part of.

The thing that stands out the most is the strong sense of community.

Everything is connected by the pennant banner. This shows the connection and sense of community unique to Mayofest. The look is classic to Mayofest, but representative of the kind of atmosphere expected at this festive event.

Besides trying to capture the character of Mayofest, it’s also important viewers know right away what the main attractions are. These are visualized in the A and the Y of the logo, showing a tent and a guitar making it very clear that this is the place to be for camping and great music.

Professionally versatile, cheeky for insiders

As a not-for-profit, it is important that the brand has a strong professional appearance. Mayofest is is on the rise and getting bigger every year, this means they need to look the part for potential investors.

There is a very dedicated community behind Mayofest, and they have a very saucy sense of humour. Many would call it crass, and it's definitely vulgar. Their motto—which is written on staff shirts—is "Don't #$%! up!"

This one of a kind community, even though they are getting a more mature logo, needed to keep that true sense of Mayofest. This is where the concept of the inside joke came in. A true Mayofester would love the MF'R merchandise. Or staff is now MF'N Staff, these cheeky double entendres are the kind of humour that makes Mayofest and the Mayofesters who attend unique.

Let there be merch

As a not-for-profit, it’s important that the merch is as exciting as the event itself. This way, Mayofest is getting the funds they need to keep expanding, and at the same time people are excited about the merch they get to take home.

One of the most important pieces of merchandise available at events is the non-glass drinking container. Since glass isn’t allowed on the grounds, people are encouraged to bring their own cups.

Since Mayofesters are environmentally conscious, the perfect keepsake to any Mayo event would be the traditional camping mug or cup.

Mayofest's new brand keeps the same feeling you'll find in this community, but it opens new doors to future sponsors and expansion for the event.
